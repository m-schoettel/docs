Apparatus

1r2 pu(s)padanta • It would also be possible to read puṣpadanta. However, other instances of that name in this epigraph are consistently spelled with a dental s. — 1r2 m(uk)utha- • Understand mukuṭā-?
1v1 vvantən • A second akṣara na is written above the ligature nta. The engraver would probably have realised that he was beginning to write too high, and stopped after the first akṣara, then rewrote nta at the proper place.

Translation into English by Marine Schoettel

In Śaka 1261, month of Māgha, ninth lunar day of the waning fortnight, Mavulu, Umanis, Sunday / Tuesday, [the vuku of] Vuyai: such was the time that rakryan chief Puspadanta, mukuthavarṣa, won the lawsuit against Mpu Sāvitra. [It] had been judged by all the ministers of Tumapəl, with the interpreters [of dharma] (upapatti). The situation of Mpu Sāvitra was to speak first (lit. "have the beginning", makaprasthāva), rashly accusing rakryan chief Puspadanta of having seized the land of Kabhagavanan (or “the place of the hermits”), of ten lirih’s worth. Such was the case of Mpu Sāvitra. As for the case of the rakryan chief Puspadanta: “the land, it is his possession (lit. possessed by him) from his paternal lineage/ancestors, [measuring] ten lirih.” Thus did contest those who were solicited for right knowledge.
